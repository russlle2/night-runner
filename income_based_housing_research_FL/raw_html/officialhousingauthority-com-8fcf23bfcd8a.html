Lake County Housing Authority


The Lake County Housing Authority oversees both public housing and voucher programs, if available. The housing authority owns and manages facilities which total units across the city. Public housing agencies, including the public housing agency in Lake County, are required to submit and receive approval from the US Department of Housing and Urban Development Jacksonville Area Office every 5 years. They detail the housing authority's strategy, outreach programs, operations, and policies so information can change. The Lake County HA code is FL106, and it's part of the Jacksonville Area Office.

Helping You Find Cheap Apartments in Lake County

The Lake County Housing Authority offers a wide variety of programs ranging from affordable public housing, housing projects, and subsidized housing all over Lake County, FL. The staff's goals include providing affordable housing in secure environments, while providing a high level of service. You can browse available apartments, housing units, and other accommodations directly by visiting their website:

www.hud.gov

Affordable housing in Lake County and elsewhere means wither rental or owner-occupied housing that costs at or less than 30% of one's gross income. Subsidized housing means a residence that the government assists in paying for. It can either be all or a portion of the monthly payment, depending on the person's income.

Lake County Housing Authority Application

If you're looking for apply for affordable housing in Lake County, you'll have to fill out an application (if the housing authority is currently accepting new applicants. We encourage you to first check on the status of new applicants by visiting the Lake County Housing Authority website:


www.hud.gov

Once you've determined that you've determined they are accepting new applications for public housing in Lake County, FL, you can begin your application process. The fiscal year ends on 30-Sep, so we recommend avoiding applying during that period as you might experience delays. Your eligibility for these programs depends on the following:

  • Annual gross income
  • Age or disability status
  • US citizenship or eligible immigration

The Lake County Housing Authority will check references that you put on your application to make sure that you will be a good addition to the housing project. Once your application is finished, you can send it to the mailing address which is:
